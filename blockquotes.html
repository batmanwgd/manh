Blockquotes are indicators,
that the selected text is an extended quotation.

Blockquotes indicate that the enclosed text is an extended quotation. A URL for the source of the quote may be given using the cite attribute, while a simple text representaion of the source can be given by using the HTML <cite> element.

If the quotation is in a language other than that of the main document, lang= and maybe dir= attributes may be relevant to specify the language of the quoted text and perhaps its direction, left-to-right or right-to-left.
In HTML and XHTML, the blockquote element defines "a section [within a document] that is quoted from another source".
The syntax is:
<blockquote>
    <p>blockquoted text goes here<p>
<blockquote>
The blockquote element is used to indicate the quotation of a large section of text from another source.

Example

“There are three responses to a piece of design – yes, no, and WOW! Wow is the one to aim for.”
